{% trans "Gulyás (Goulash)" %}

{% blocktrans %} Gulyás is one of Hungary’s most famous dishes. It is a hearty stew made with meat, vegetables, and paprika, which gives it its distinctive flavor and vibrant red color. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} It is typically prepared with beef, onions, paprika, and vegetables such as potatoes and carrots. It is often served with bread or noodles and is considered a classic comfort food. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Pörkölt" %}

{% blocktrans %} Pörkölt is a traditional Hungarian stew made with pork, beef, or chicken, slow-cooked with onions, vegetables, and paprika. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} It is similar to goulash but usually has a thicker consistency and less broth. It is commonly served with bread or noodles. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} The name "pörkölt" means "roasted," referring to the cooking method. The dish is known for its rich and savory flavor. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Lángos" %}

{% blocktrans %} Lángos is a popular Hungarian street food made from deep-fried dough, typically topped with garlic, sour cream, and cheese. {% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Csirke Paprikás" %}

{% blocktrans %} Csirke Paprikás is a beloved Hungarian dish made with chicken cooked in a flavorful paprika-based sauce. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} It is known for its rich and creamy texture and is often served at both casual meals and festive occasions. {% endblocktrans %}
