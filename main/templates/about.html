{% trans "Geography of Hungary" %}

{% blocktrans %} Roughly the size of the state of Indiana, Hungary is a landlocked country in Central Europe bordered by Slovakia and Austria to the north, Ukraine and Romania to the east, Slovenia to the west, and Croatia and Serbia to the south. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} Hungary is mostly flat, with a vast lowland area known as the Great Hungarian Plain, a productive agricultural region. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} The Danube River runs through the middle of the country and is Hungary’s largest river. It flows for nearly 1,800 miles (2,900 kilometers), passing through 10 nations on its way to the Black Sea. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} Hungary also contains three major freshwater lakes, including Lake Balaton, the largest lake in Central Europe. Lake Hévíz is also notable as one of the largest thermal lakes in the world. {% endblocktrans %}
